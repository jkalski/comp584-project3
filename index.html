All About Dog Breeds

Dogs have been humanity's companions for over 15,000 years, evolving alongside us into hundreds of distinct breeds. From the tiny Chihuahua to the massive Great Dane, each breed carries a unique history, set of traits, and purpose, whether herding livestock, hunting game, guarding homes, or simply providing love.

Today, organizations like the American Kennel Club recognize over 200 breeds grouped into categories like Sporting, Herding, Working, and Toy. Understanding a breed's origins helps owners provide the right environment, exercise, and training for a happy and healthy dog.

The Bond Between Humans and Dogs

Dogs were the first animals ever domesticated by humans, and that ancient partnership has only grown stronger. They serve as guide dogs for the visually impaired, search and rescue heroes, loyal police partners, and most importantly, unconditional companions. Science confirms what dog owners have always known. The simple act of petting a dog lowers cortisol levels and raises oxytocin in both human and dog alike.
